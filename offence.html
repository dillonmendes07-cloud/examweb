Examination Offences and Punishments

This section explains various examination-related offences and the disciplinary actions that may be taken to ensure fairness, discipline, and academic ethics.

Possession of Copying Material

Possession of any copying material such as notes, chits, books, printed matter, or any unauthorized reference material inside the examination hall is treated as a serious examination offence, irrespective of whether the material is actually used during the examination. Such possession is considered an attempt to adopt unfair means and compromises the integrity and fairness of the examination process.

In such cases, the performance of the learner at the College Examination shall be annulled in full, resulting in cancellation of the entire examination attempt. Further disciplinary action may be taken as per institutional rules.

Possession of Copying Material with Actual Copying

Possession of any copying material such as written notes, printed matter, textbooks, chits, or any unauthorized reference material inside the examination hall, along with actual copying or attempting to copy from such material, is considered a serious violation of examination rules. Such conduct undermines academic integrity and compromises the fairness of the examination process. This offence applies irrespective of the quantity of material possessed and includes any attempt to gain unfair advantage during the examination.

The learner shall be excluded from the College Examinations concerned for one subsequent examination.

Mutual / Mass copying

Mutual or mass copying refers to a situation where two or more learners are found to be copying from one another or are collectively involved in the exchange of answers or information during the examination. Such behaviour may include sharing answer sheets, communicating answers verbally or through signals, or any coordinated attempt by a group of learners to gain unfair advantage. Mutual or mass copying is considered a grave offence as it directly compromises the integrity, fairness, and credibility of the examination process.

All learners involved in mutual or mass copying shall be excluded from the College Examinations concerned for two subsequent examinations, along with any further disciplinary action as deemed appropriate by the Examination Committee.

Impersonation

Impersonation refers to appearing for an examination on behalf of another learner or allowing another individual to appear in one’s place in any College or other examination. Such acts involve deliberate misrepresentation of identity and constitute a serious breach of examination rules and academic integrity. Impersonation undermines the credibility of the examination system and is regarded as a grave form of examination malpractice.

The learner shall be excluded from the College Examinations concerned for five subsequent examinations. In cases where the impersonator is also a learner of the College, both learners involved shall be subject to the same punishment, along with any further disciplinary action as decided by the Examination Committee.

Tampering with Answer Books or Documents

Interfering with, tampering, or counterfeiting the College seal, answer books, or any official office stationery used in the conduct of examinations, with the intention of misleading the examination authorities, is considered a grave and premeditated offence. Such actions include falsification of official documents, misuse of institutional seals, or manipulation of examination materials to gain unfair advantage. This offence directly undermines the credibility, authenticity, and integrity of the examination system and is viewed as a serious breach of institutional trust and discipline.

The learner shall be excluded from the College Examinations concerned for four subsequent examinations, in addition to any further disciplinary action as decided by the Examination Committee.

Misconduct and Indiscipline

Using obscene, abusive, or threatening language, or issuing violent threats inside the examination hall by a learner towards the room supervisor, invigilator, or any other examination authority is considered a serious act of misconduct. Such behaviour disrupts the examination environment, creates fear or intimidation, and undermines the authority of examination personnel. This offence includes verbal abuse, aggressive conduct, or any form of threatening behaviour intended to influence or obstruct the fair conduct of the examination.

The learner shall be excluded from the College Examinations concerned for four subsequent examinations, in addition to any further disciplinary action as decided by the Examination Committee.

Insertion of currency notes/ bribing or attempt to bribe

Insertion of currency notes or offering monetary benefits, including any attempt to bribe a person connected with the conduct of the examination such as an invigilator, supervisor, or other examination authority, is considered a serious act of examination misconduct. Such behaviour compromises the fairness and integrity of the examination process and is treated as a grave violation of institutional discipline.

The learner shall be excluded from the College Examinations concerned for four subsequent examinations. Any money involved in the offence shall be confiscated and credited to the Learners’ Welfare Fund, as per institutional rules.

Strict disciplinary measures ensure fairness and academic ethics.

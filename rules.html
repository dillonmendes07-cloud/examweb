Examination Rules

The following examination rules are framed with reference to official undergraduate and postgraduate examination guidelines and are applicable to all students appearing for examinations.

Eligibility and Attendance Requirements

Students are permitted to appear for examinations only if they have fulfilled the minimum attendance and internal assessment requirements prescribed by the institution. Failure to meet eligibility conditions may result in disqualification from appearing for the examination.

Examination Registration and Documentation

Students must complete the examination registration process within the prescribed timeline. Carrying a valid hall ticket and college identity card is mandatory. Incomplete or incorrect documentation may lead to denial of entry into the examination hall.

Reporting Time and Seating Arrangement

Students must report to the examination hall at least fifteen minutes prior to the commencement of the examination. Seating arrangements must be followed strictly as displayed or instructed by invigilators. Changing seats without permission is not allowed.

Conduct and Discipline During Examination

Students are expected to maintain silence, discipline, and decorum throughout the examination. Any form of communication, disturbance, or non-cooperation with invigilators is treated as misconduct.

Answer Book Usage and Writing Instructions

Answers must be written only in the answer books and supplementary sheets provided by the institution. Writing names, roll numbers, symbols, or any identifying marks on the answer book is strictly prohibited. Any attempt to alter or damage answer books is a serious offence.

Prohibited Materials and Use of Unfair Means

Possession or use of unauthorized materials such as written notes, books, electronic gadgets, or communication devices is strictly forbidden. Even possession without usage may be treated as adoption of unfair means under examination rules.

Absenteeism and Incomplete Examination

Students who are absent for an examination or leave the examination incomplete due to any reason shall be treated as absent. Such cases are governed by institutional rules regarding reappearance or evaluation.

Submission of Answer Books and Exit Procedure

Answer books must be submitted to the invigilator before leaving the examination hall. Students must follow proper exit procedures as instructed. Leaving the hall without submitting answer books may result in disciplinary action.

Evaluation, Results, and Rechecking

Evaluation of answer books is conducted as per prescribed academic standards. Students may apply for revaluation or verification of marks within the stipulated time frame, subject to institutional rules and procedures.

Authority of Examination Committee

The Examination Committee and designated authorities reserve the right to interpret examination rules and take appropriate decisions in cases of ambiguity or violation. Decisions taken by the committee shall be final and binding on the student.

Students are advised to strictly adhere to examination rules to ensure fairness, transparency, and academic integrity.
